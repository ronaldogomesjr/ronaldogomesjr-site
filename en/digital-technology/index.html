digital technology

Digital technology is understood as infrastructure, language and social practice that mediates learning, communication and acts of authorship. More than tools, devices, networks and platforms constitute sociotechnical ecosystems that reorganise time, space and ways of participating, collaborating and making meaning.

Perspective

When digital technology is understood as a sociotechnical ecology, we expand our capacity to create critical, ethical and inclusive educational experiences that cultivate autonomy, computational thinking, networked collaboration and responsible authorship.
